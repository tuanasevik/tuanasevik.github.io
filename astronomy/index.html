Astronomy

Astronomy is a natural science that studies celestial objects and phenomena.

It uses mathematics, physics, and chemistry in order to explain their origin and evolution.

A planet is a large astronomical body that is neither a star nor a stellar remnant.

Objects of interest include planets, moons, stars, nebulae, galaxies, and comets.

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter.

Stars are the most widely recognized astronomical objects, and represent the most fundamental building blocks of galaxies. The age, distribution, and composition of the stars in a galaxy trace the history, dynamics, and evolution of that galaxy.
